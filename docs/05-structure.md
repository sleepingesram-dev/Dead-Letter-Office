# 05 — Story Structure & Endings

## Game Structure

The game is divided into shifts.

Ideal full game length:

- 2 to 4 hours for a normal playthrough
- 4 to 6 hours for completionists

Number of shifts:

- 7 main shifts
- Optional final shift
- Secret epilogue shift

Each shift introduces:

- New letters
- One main story reveal
- One mechanic or tool
- One office change
- One optional secret chain
- One new rule complication

## Shift Breakdown

### Shift 1: Orientation

**Goal:** Introduce normal mail sorting. Player receives basic letters from Marrow Creek.

**Gameplay:**

- Learn how to inspect mail.
- Sort valid vs invalid mail.
- Use address registry.
- Stamp decisions.

**Tone:** Mostly mundane but slightly off.

**Key weird moment:** One letter has tomorrow's date.

**Main reveal:** Marrow Creek does not exist in the official address database.

**End event:** The office printer outputs: "Do not correct the database."

### Shift 2: Deceased Recipients

**Goal:** Introduce death records and moral conflict.

**Gameplay:**

- Check letters against death records.
- Decide whether mail to dead recipients should be destroyed.
- Learn about Ruth Bell and Jonah Bell.

**Key weird moment:** A destroyed letter reappears in the incoming tray, burned around the edges.

**Main reveal:** Some dead recipients keep receiving mail.

**End event:** The wall map adds Bell House.

### Shift 3: Contradictory Dates

**Goal:** Introduce timeline contradictions.

**Gameplay:**

- Verify dates.
- Compare letters across years.
- Use newspaper archive.

**Key weird moment:** A letter references an event that only happens if the player delivered a previous letter.

**Main reveal:** Player actions may be changing the town's history.

**End event:** Phone rings. A child asks: "Did you send it yet?"

### Shift 4: Black-Sealed Mail

**Goal:** Introduce forbidden letters.

**Gameplay:**

- Black-sealed envelopes are not supposed to be opened.
- Opening them reveals hidden files.
- Obeying the rule preserves compliance but blocks truth.

**Key weird moment:** One black-sealed letter contains a photo of the player's desk from that exact moment.

**Main reveal:** The office has processed the player before.

**End event:** Supervisor's office unlocks.

### Shift 5: The Previous Clerk

**Goal:** Reveal the predecessor's hidden notes.

**Gameplay:**

- Search supervisor office.
- Decode previous clerk's filing system.
- Learn how to hide letters from the office.

**Key weird moment:** The previous clerk's name changes every time it appears.

**Main reveal:** Every clerk who discovers the truth becomes undeliverable.

**End event:** Player finds an employee file with their own name, mostly redacted.

### Shift 6: The Delivery Chute

**Goal:** Introduce reality-changing deliveries.

**Gameplay:**

- Some letters can be physically delivered through the chute.
- Deliveries alter map, records, and later letters.
- The player must choose which memories to restore.

**Key weird moment:** After delivering a letter to the schoolteacher, children's drawings appear in the break room.

**Main reveal:** The town still exists somewhere, held together by mail.

**End event:** Basement archive unlocks.

### Shift 7: The Basement Archive

**Goal:** Reveal the truth of Marrow Creek.

**Gameplay:**

- Process oldest letters.
- Decode founding records.
- Find the first dead letter.
- Choose whether to restore or permanently erase the town.

**Key weird moment:** Letters are now written in the player's handwriting.

**Main reveal:** The player was from Marrow Creek, or was created from its remaining records.

**End event:** Final choice opens.

### Final Shift: Return to Sender

**Goal:** Ending sequence. Player decides what happens to the town, the office, and themselves.

Possible final actions:

- Deliver the first letter.
- Burn the archive.
- Archive yourself.
- Return every letter to sender.
- Refuse to process anything.
- Mail yourself to Marrow Creek.

## Endings

There are 4 main endings and 1 secret ending.

### Ending 1: Compliance Ending

**How to get:**

- Follow most office rules.
- Destroy forbidden letters.
- Report contradictions.
- Avoid personal memory recovery.

**Result:** The town remains erased. The player receives a promotion. Their name disappears from the office door.

**Tone:** Bleak. The player survives by becoming part of the system.

**Final image:** A new clerk arrives. The player's processed file is waiting on the desk.

### Ending 2: Memory Ending

**How to get:**

- Archive important letters.
- Preserve character stories.
- Recover enough memories.
- Disobey some rules.

**Result:** Marrow Creek is remembered, but not restored. The office collapses as records flood back into the world.

**Tone:** Bittersweet.

**Final image:** A public memorial appears where the town used to be.

### Ending 3: Delivery Ending

**How to get:**

- Deliver key letters through the chute.
- Restore locations on the map.
- Help Ruth, Eleanor, and Jonah's storylines.

**Result:** Marrow Creek returns, but changed. Some people come back wrong.

**Tone:** Unsettling victory.

**Final image:** The player receives a postcard from Marrow Creek with no stamp.

### Ending 4: Burn Ending

**How to get:**

- Destroy many letters.
- Use incinerator heavily.
- Reject both office and town.

**Result:** The office burns. The town is freed from records, but all evidence is lost.

**Tone:** Ambiguous.

**Final image:** Ash falls like snow. A single letter survives.

### Secret Ending: Return to Sender

**How to get:**

- Find all previous clerk notes.
- Decode the first letter.
- Refuse the final sorting categories.
- Place the player's own employee file into Return to Sender.

**Result:** The player is sent back to Marrow Creek before the erasure.

**Tone:** Strange, haunting, hopeful.

**Final image:** A mailbox opens in daylight. Inside is the first letter, unopened.
