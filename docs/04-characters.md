# 04 — Characters

The characters are mostly experienced through letters, records, photos, and hidden documents.

## 1. The Player Character

The player is unnamed at first.

Possible late-game reveal — the player may be:

- A former resident of Marrow Creek
- A child who survived the town's erasure
- A previous clerk whose memory was reset
- The intended final recipient of the dead letters
- Someone created by the office to process unresolved mail

Avoid overexplaining. The player should discover disturbing implications.

## 2. Eleanor Vale

A schoolteacher from Marrow Creek.

**Public role:**

- Kind teacher
- Wrote letters to former students
- Tried to preserve town history

**Hidden truth:**

- She knew the town was being erased.
- She taught children to hide memories inside letters.
- She may have sent the first impossible letter.

**Emotional role:** memory, care, resistance.

## 3. Silas Crowe

Former postmaster of Marrow Creek.

**Public role:**

- Town postmaster
- Rule-following bureaucrat

**Hidden truth:**

- Helped the government classify residents as "undeliverable."
- Later regretted it.
- Hid evidence inside sorting codes.

**Emotional role:** guilt and cowardice.

## 4. Ruth Bell

A mother searching for her missing son.

**Public role:**

- Sent dozens of letters to her son after he disappeared.

**Hidden truth:**

- Her son was one of the first erased people.
- Her letters kept bringing traces of him back.
- The office marked her as dangerous.

**Emotional role:** grief and denial.

## 5. Jonah Bell

Ruth's missing son.

**Public role:**

- Missing child.

**Hidden truth:**

- May have become trapped inside the mail system.
- His handwriting appears across decades.
- Some letters from him are addressed to the player.

**Emotional role:** innocence and the cost of forgetting.

## 6. Director Harlan Pike

Government official connected to the erasure program.

**Public role:**

- Never appears directly at first.
- Signs official policy documents.

**Hidden truth:**

- Created the Dead Letter Office.
- Believed memory itself could contaminate reality.
- May still be alive, or preserved through documents.

**Emotional role:** institutional cruelty.

## 7. The Previous Clerk

A former employee who left hidden notes.

**Public role:**

- The player's predecessor.

**Hidden truth:**

- They discovered too much.
- Their name has been scratched out of every record.
- Their notes help the player break rules.

**Emotional role:** warning and obsession.
