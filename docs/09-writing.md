# 09 — Writing

## Writing Style

The writing should be:

- Plain
- Human
- Specific
- Sad
- Creepy through implication
- Bureaucratic when official
- Emotional when personal
- Never overwritten

Avoid purple prose.

**Bad:**

> "The abyss of memory swallowed my soul beneath the crimson moon."

**Good:**

> "I set a plate for him again. I know I should stop."

Official documents should be cold and inhuman:

> "Residents unable to maintain valid civic continuity are to be classified as undeliverable."

Personal letters should be grounded and intimate:

> "You left your blue scarf in the hall. I keep moving it back to your chair."

**The horror should come from the contrast between human letters and cold systems.**

## Example Official Policies

### Policy 14-A: Deceased Recipients

All mail addressed to deceased recipients must be destroyed unless otherwise marked by supervisory authority.

Clerks are not permitted to preserve, copy, discuss, or personally respond to such mail.

### Policy 22-C: Invalid Municipalities

Mail addressed to non-recognized municipalities must be archived without correction.

Clerks are not permitted to add, restore, or verify unofficial place names.

### Policy 31-F: Memory Contamination

Documents containing unauthorized recollection phrases must be flagged.

Restricted phrases include:

- I remember
- Come home
- We were here
- Do not let them forget
- Return to sender
- The town is awake

### Policy 40-D: Clerk Reference

Any document referencing the current clerk by name, description, or circumstance is to be treated as misaddressed.

Clerks are advised not to personalize processing irregularities.
