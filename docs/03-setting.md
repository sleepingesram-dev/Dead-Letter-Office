# 03 — Setting

## The Dead Letter Office

The game takes place mostly in one building: a forgotten government mail facility located somewhere outside normal maps.

The office should feel:

- Old
- Fluorescent-lit
- Damp
- Quiet
- Institutional
- Too large for one employee
- Filled with locked rooms
- Designed by people who did not expect to be questioned

### Main Areas

#### 1. Sorting Desk

The player's main workstation. Contains:

- Incoming mail tray
- Outgoing sorted bins
- Stamps
- Magnifying glass
- Letter opener
- UV lamp
- Date verifier
- Address registry terminal
- Case file drawer
- Policy manual
- Desk lamp
- Personal notes
- Shift checklist

#### 2. Wall Map of Marrow Creek

A large town map that updates over time. At first, many places are missing or blacked out.

Map locations include:

- Church
- School
- Motel
- Post office
- Flood canal
- Train station
- Coal road
- Orchard
- Cemetery
- Radio tower
- Town hall
- House on Briar Lane
- Unmarked district

The map should be used for clue tracking, not just decoration.

#### 3. Filing Room

Rows of cabinets containing old records. Some drawers are locked until the player discovers names, dates, or file codes.

#### 4. Archive Room

Stores processed letters. The player can reread old letters and notice new changes.

#### 5. Incinerator Room

Used to destroy letters marked as forbidden or dangerous. Burning certain letters has story consequences.

#### 6. Delivery Chute

A strange locked chute where certain letters can be "delivered" even though the town is gone. When the player uses it, the world changes.

#### 7. Break Room

Small room with vending machine, old radio, employee lockers, and bulletin board. Used for environmental storytelling.

#### 8. Supervisor's Office

Initially locked. Contains information about previous clerks, erased employees, and the true purpose of the office.

#### 9. Basement Archive

Late-game area. Contains the oldest mail, pre-town records, and letters that should never have been written.

## The Town: Marrow Creek

Marrow Creek is the erased town at the center of the story. Official records say it never existed. The letters prove otherwise.

Possible history:

- Founded as a mining or railway town.
- Suffered a disaster decades ago.
- The government erased it from records.
- Survivors were relocated or made to forget.
- The post office continued receiving mail after the town vanished.
- The Dead Letter Office was built to contain the town's memory.

The town's central mystery:

> Marrow Creek was not destroyed because something happened there. It was destroyed because something there kept remembering things that reality wanted forgotten.

The town should feel like a place the player reconstructs through mail, maps, names, and contradictions — not a place they walk through. The town exists through documents, map changes, sounds, and imagination. That is cheaper and scarier than building it as an explorable world.
