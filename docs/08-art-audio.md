# 08 — Art & Audio

## Visual Identity

The visual style should be:

- Dark
- Minimal
- Analog
- Bureaucratic
- Tactile
- Slightly surreal

Art direction:

- 3D low-poly or stylized realistic
- Limited environment
- Strong lighting
- Heavy shadows
- Dirty paper textures
- Old government office colors
- Muted greens, yellows, browns, grays
- Occasional red stamps and black seals

Important visual motifs:

- Redacted text
- Rubber stamps
- Manila folders
- Faded maps
- Old fluorescent lights
- Water stains
- Postmarks
- Black wax seals
- Filing cabinets
- Burn marks
- Handwritten notes
- Address labels
- Torn photographs

The game should look good in screenshots even with simple assets.

### Steam Capsule

The capsule should show:

- A desk covered in letters
- One black envelope
- Red stamp reading "UNDELIVERABLE"
- Dark office background
- Maybe a town map behind the desk
- Strong title typography

## Audio Identity

Audio is critical. The game should use:

- Fluorescent buzzing
- Paper rustling
- Rubber stamp sounds
- Distant pipes
- Old building creaks
- Rain against windows
- Distant phone rings
- Low radio static
- Drawer scraping
- Incinerator hum
- Clock ticking
- Tape recorder clicks
- Muffled voices, very rarely

Music should be minimal. Use drones, soft piano fragments, tape warble, and low industrial tones.

The player should often hear nothing but office ambience.

**When something strange happens, audio should change subtly before visuals do.**
