# 06 — Letters

## Letter Design Rules

Every letter should have:

- Sender
- Recipient
- Date
- Address
- Surface meaning
- Hidden meaning
- Mechanical purpose
- Story purpose
- Possible player decision
- Consequence

A good letter should do at least 2 of these:

- Reveal character
- Create contradiction
- Unlock a location
- Hint at a code
- Change how a previous letter is understood
- Give the player a choice
- Increase dread
- Add emotional weight
- Connect to another character
- Hint at the player's identity

Avoid filler letters. **If a letter does not matter, remove it.**

## Example Letters

### Letter Example 1: Ruth to Jonah

**Recipient:** Jonah Bell, 12 Briar Lane, Marrow Creek
**Sender:** Ruth Bell, 12 Briar Lane, Marrow Creek
**Date:** October 3, 1962

**Body:**

> Jonah,
>
> I left your supper on the stove again. I know your father says I should stop doing that, but I heard you in the hall last night.
>
> Three knocks. Then one. Just like you used to do.
>
> If this reaches you, come home before the rain starts.
>
> Mom

**Hidden clue:** The death record says Jonah died in 1958.

**Gameplay choice:** Destroy because recipient is dead, archive as contradiction, or deliver through chute.

**Consequence:** Delivering it unlocks Bell House on the map.

### Letter Example 2: Eleanor to Former Students

**Recipient:** The Children of Room 4, Marrow Creek School
**Sender:** Eleanor Vale
**Date:** June 8, 1960

**Body:**

> If you remember the song, do not write it down.
>
> If you write it down, do not mail it.
>
> If you mail it, do not use your real name.
>
> If a clerk reads this, please forgive us. We were only trying to stay.

**Hidden clue:** First letters of each sentence form "I I I I" at first, but the text changes after other letters are archived.

**Gameplay choice:** Archive, flag, or destroy.

**Consequence:** Archiving unlocks children's drawings in the break room.

### Letter Example 3: Unknown to Player

**Recipient:** Current Clerk, Dead Letter Office
**Sender:** You
**Date:** Tomorrow

**Body:**

> Do not open the black envelopes.
>
> You already did.

**Hidden clue:** The player has processed these letters before.

**Gameplay choice:** Can be hidden in personal drawer.

**Consequence:** Unlocks previous clerk storyline.
